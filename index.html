A complete blood count (CBC) is used to evaluate overall health and detect wide range of disorders, including anemia, infection and leukemia.
There have been some reports of WBC and platelet counts being lower in venous blood than in capillary blood samples, although still within these reference ranges. Low WBC - sepsis, marrow hypoplasia
High WBC - acute stress, infection, malignancies

Low platelets - risk of bleeding

High platelets - risk of thrombosis


Notes :

1.Macrocytic Anemia/Dimorphic Anemia can have low platelet count.

2.Microcytic Anemia/Leucocytosis can have Reactive thrombocytosis.

For microcytic indices a Mentzer index of less than 13 suggests that the patient may have thalassemia trait, and an index of more than 13 suggests that the patient may have iron deficiency.
